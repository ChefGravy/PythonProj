My Favorite Neighborhoods:

Having been raised in Seattle since the early '90s, I've had a front row seat to this city's transformation into the tech giant that it is today. Currently Seattle is undergoing an extensive change spurred by the success of Amazon, which has now surpassed Microsoft as the richest publicly owned company in the world.

As a result, Seattle is assuming its place among major vibrant, energetic metropolises like New York City and Los Angeles. With this growth, the city offers increased amentities for attendees and highlights its best offerings.

Ballard:
Biscuit & Bean, Reuben's Brews, Gracia, Stone Gardens, Ballard Locks, Trailbend Taprooms, Ballard Market, are just a few names that I think about when someone says, "Let's go to Ballard". I can spend an entire day roaming around Ballard and still not see everything. When I was a kid, my parents toook me to the Ballard Locks during the summer and I got to see the Pacific Salmon pass through the Locks. If you were lucky, you got to see a seal on the hunt for some Salmon. On the weekends, my wife and I will wake up early...ish and make our way to the Ballard Market where buy all our fresh ingredients for the week. If you're hungry and skipped breakfast, you can always grab a bite from any number of food vendors that are cooking that day.

Fremont:
I remember the first time I saw the Fremont Troll. I was just a kid when my dad slowly drove up 36th and all of a sudden, he said, "Hey, what's that?" pointing at the troll. I didn't know what to think, it was huge! Back then the VW wasn't filled with cement, the city only did that so nobody would get hurt while crawling inside. From that day on, any time someone said Fremont, I thought of that Troll. But as much as I love the Fremont Troll, there is far more to Fremont: Fremont Brewing Company, Schilling Cider House, Fainting Goat Gelato, Theo Chocolate, Jive Time Records, Portage Bay Goods, and Milstead & Co. I love Fremont even more because you're a hop, skip, and a jump from Gas Works Park which is absolutely amazing during the summer. and if you love running or biking, the Burke-Gilman Trail, also known as the Burke, is right around the corner.

Queen Anne: Queen Anne Market
If there is one neighborhood in the Seattle that has changed more than any other, it's probably going to be Queen Anne. I know this because I went to middle school there: New Options Middle School, also knows as just N.O.M.S. This was back in the mid '90s and I'll never forget it. The school is no longer there but the building remains. You can read more about it here; it was actually built around 1862. I love Queen Anne because it sits on top of a hill and is super close to the Space Needle, Chihuly Garden and Glass, and Seattle Center. The reason this is important is because once a year during about the third week of the month of July, this happens, The Bite of Seattle and it's at the Seattle Center. It is arguably one of my favorite events of the year and I make every effort to go. I love the entertainment, the food, the beer, and all the attractions this event brings. If you've never been, I highly recommend it.

Greenwood: Greenlake/Greenwood Market Greenwood/LoyalHeights Market
Known as the area around 85th Street and Greenwood Avenue is booming with coffeehouses, antique stores, bars, and eateries. One of my favorite restaurants to visit is Flintcreek Cattle and is located on corner of 85th and Greenwood Ave N. Once a year, Greenwood hosts the Greenwood Car Show that happens every June and the Seafair Parade in August. If you love classic cars, the Car Show is definitely the event for you. Right after the Car Show you can head over to Chucks' Hop Shop, proudly deemed "The Land of a Thousand Beers". It also features a rotating schedule of daily food trucks - making it the perfect spot for a quick bite and a beer! And if you have a sweet tooth, don't forget the homemade truffles at Chocolati Cafe.
